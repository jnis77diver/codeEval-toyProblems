NOW OPEN FOR ENROLLMENT !
 

The award-winning HEALERS ART COURSE in resiliency and professionalism is now taught annually in 8o+ medical schools nationwide and seven countries abroad. More than 17,000 medical students have successfully completed the course. This innovative Discovery Model curriculum offers students a personal exploration of service intention and healing relationship, and strengthens their ability to find meaning and satisfaction in daily work despite the pressures and demands of medical training and practice. 

 

Outcomes research has been published in the Journal of General Internal Medicine, Annals of Family Medicine, Academic Medicine, Family Medicine, and Teaching and Learning in Medicine. For more information on published peer-reviewed articles on the outcomes of the course, click here. 

 

MORE ABOUT THE COURSE   

Founded in 1991 at UCSF School of Medicine, the  Healer's Art Course is a
15-hr/5 session elective for first and second year medical students.
The course integrates formative principles and approaches drawn from
such diverse fields as contemplative studies, humanistic and transpersonal psychology, spiritual direction, formation education, poetry, aesthetics, literature and narrative, guided reflection, journal writing, and adult education into a powerful Discovery Model learning experience.    

 
MORE ABOUT THE FACULTY DEVELOPMENT TRAINING  

The 5-day Training is an immersion experience into this unique curriculum in resiliency, professionalism, formation education, ethics and mindfulness. During the FACULTY TRAINING workshop you will join with academics from across the United States to personally experience the transformative aspects of the Healer's Art Course, as well as master the principles of the curriculum. The Training provides faculty with the tools, materials and strategic thinking necessary to successfully implement the course at their own institutions. Once the training is completed, participants will have access to ISHI staff for ongoing free consultation. More than 95% of participants have successfully implemented the course at their medical schools.
